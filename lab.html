The International Reagent Resource (IRR)is distributing the diagnostic panel and diagnostic supplies to registered state and local public health laboratories so they can perform SARS-CoV-2 testing.

During the SARS-CoV-2 pandemic, state public health laboratories are authorizing county or city laboratories in each state. These laboratories must be certified under the Clinical Laboratory Improvement Amendment (CLIA) to perform high complexity tests, have appropriate laboratory equipment and training, and demonstrate testing proficiency under their state laboratory’s stewardship in order to maintain their status as an IRR registered laboratory. IRR does not supply clinicians, hospitals, and health care professionals with testing kits directly at this time. Clinicians, hospitals, and health care professionals should refer to the list of commercially available lots of primers and probes that have passed CDC functional quality control.

    The CDC 2019-nCoV Real-Time RT-PCR Diagnostic Panelcontains the following items:

  • T2019-nCoV_N1 and 2019-nCoV_N2 primers and probes that target regions of the virus nucleocapsid (N) gene. The panel is designed for specific detection of SARS-CoV-2.
  • RP primers and probes that target the Human RNase P gene.
  • nCoVPC, the 2019-nCoV positive control used in the assay.

The CDC 2019-nCoV Real-Time RT-PCR Diagnostic Panel requires these control materials, or other authorized control materials listed below. All of these controls must generate expected results for a test to be considered valid, as outlined in the CDC 2019-nCoV Real-Time RT-PCR Diagnostic Panel Instructions for Use

  • Human Specimen Control (HSC): A human cell culture preparation used as an extraction procedural control to demonstrate successful recovery of nucleic acid, as well as extraction reagent integrity.

  • No Template Control (NTC): Nuclease-free water included in each run. Monitors for reagent and system contamination.
